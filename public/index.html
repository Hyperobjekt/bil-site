Some things to know
about test scores:

Average test scores reveal the amount of educational opportunity in a given area...

...not the innate ability or intelligence of the children who live there. They reflect socioeconomic conditions and the quality of education kids have access to, in and out of school. These can differ greatly from place to place.

Growth rates in test scores show the degree to which kids are advancing or lagging...

...in knowledge as they move from grade to grade. They’re the best measure we have of how well schools are educating children.

Rates of improvement show how fast test scores are improving.

We use this measure to show where schools and communities are getting better at providing educational opportunities over time.

Use the interactive map to see how your community scores on all these measures, and explore patterns of educational opportunity across the US.
